6 Tips to Build a Habit
that Actually Sticks

Building a habit that sticks can be challenging, but with the right strategies, it's entirely possible.
Here are six tips to help you develop lasting habits.

1. Start Small

Tip: Begin with a tiny version of the habit you want to build. For instance, if you want to start exercising, begin with just 5 minutes a day.

Why it works: Small changes are easier to incorporate into your daily routine and help build confidence and momentum over time.

2. Be Consistent

Tip: Perform the habit at the same time and place every day.

Why it works: Consistency helps create a routine, making the habit feel automatic. It reduces the need for decision-making and builds a stable context for the habit to thrive.

3. Use Triggers

Tip: Pair your new habit with an existing habit or a specific cue. For example, if you want to meditate daily, do it right after brushing your teeth.

Why it works: Triggers act as reminders and help integrate the new habit into your existing routine, making it easier to remember and perform.

4. Track Your Progress

Tip: Keep a record of your habit. Use a journal, an app, or a simple checklist to mark each day you complete your habit.

Why it works: Tracking progress provides a visual reminder of your commitment and achievements, which can motivate you to keep going.

5. Reward Yourself

Tip: Give yourself a small reward each time you complete your habit. It could be something simple like a piece of chocolate, a few minutes of your favorite activity, or a gold star on your calendar.

Why it works: Rewards create a positive association with the habit, reinforcing your behavior and making it more enjoyable.

6. Be Patient and Forgiving

Tip: Understand that building a habit takes time and setbacks are normal. If you miss a day, don’t get discouraged—just get back on track the next day.

Why it works: Patience helps you stay committed long-term. Being forgiving with yourself prevents discouragement and allows you to view the process as a journey rather than a pass/fail test.
